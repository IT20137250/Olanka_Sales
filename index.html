1. Olanka Sales Environment Introduction

Before we jump into the Olanka Sales Process, let’s look at a few basic terms to understand what we do as a business.

  1. What is a business? A business is all about buying and selling. We will be buying from our suppliers and selling it to customers.

  2. So what do we do as a business at Olanka? We buy from our travel related global suppliers and sell it to global customers around the world.

  3. Who are Olanka Customers? Any person who wish to buy travel packages, transport services, air tickets, visa services or any kind of services related to their traveling we call them as a client Customer.

  4. There are 2 types of Olanka customers

    1. B2B (Business to Business) customer - Travel agents & Tour Operators all over the world

    2. B2C (Business to Consumer) customer - Direct travelers all over the world.

  5. Who are Olanka Suppliers?
    All kinds of service providers all over the world which are related to travel business we call them as a supplier.

    Olanka main global suppliers are

    1. Hotels & resorts

    2. DMCs (Destination management companies)

    3. Transport providers

    4. Drivers & guides

    5. Tourist activity / excursion providers

    6. Visa & immigration services

    7. Airlines & airline consolidated agents

    8. Travel Insurances companies

  6. What are Olanka products?
    At Olanka we have 3 types of travel service products.


    1. Tailor made travel arrangements (curated travel)

    2. Fix Tour Packages

    3. Small Groups (group tours/fix departure tours)

    These 3 types of different products can be from any of the destinations we operate.

  7. Currently we operate to below destinations

    2. Maldives

    3. Vietnam

    4. Indonesia

    5. Dubai

5. Customer Requirement Gathering

Customer Requirement Gathering (RG) step is one of the most important steps in our sales process. We have a lot of emphasis on this step, so please pay attention to details here. For your easier understanding of RG, we have broken it down to 3 sections. Before, During & After the RG call.



1. Before the call - Do’s & Don'ts


Do’s Dont’s

  • Prepare yourself & environment before the call.

    • Your internet connection must be good & steady.
    • You should NOT have background noises.
    • You should have a clear understanding of what questions you're going to ask. Prepare and call.
    • You must have a way to take down your notes

  • DO NOT call if you have background noises. Eg if you're in the bus or outside the roads.

  • Good practise WhatsApp message first before calling

  • Prepare yourself with questions and DO NOT call the customer for different questions at direct times. Ask all your questions in one call.

  • It’s OK to do the RG using WhatsApp text messaging if the client is comfortable. However it will be super great if you can voice call the customer if possible. It will lead to a greater “Trust” building.

  • DO NOT repeat the same questions which customers already answered to the Web Chat Team. But you can clarify, instead.


2. During the call - Do’s & Don'ts


Do’s Dont’s

  • Great & Introduce yourself. Always ask if its a good time to talk and tell how long you need for this call. Eg. 5 min.

  • If the customer is outside, or phone line is not clear DO NOT force the customer to talk, ask for good time to talk.

  • Use “Sir” “Madam” or “Mr. Mrs. Last name” when addressing the customer

  • DO NOT use your personal numbers to communicate with your leads or customers. If this happens you will be subjected to “fine” and you will be issued employment warnings & could lead to termination of your employment.

  • Speak louder , Speak Slow

  • DO NOT park seeding tour packages to the customer if you don’t have the required information. DO SEND 2 sample tour packages if you fail to get in touch with the customer in whatever scenario.

  • Double check if they can hear you clearly

  • Ask the right questions

  • Listen to the customer

  • Double check if the communication is not clear. Eg. “Sir just to double check

  • Explain about the destination if they have time to listen to you

  • Show your customer that -

    • Your knowledgeable
    • Your Professional
    • Your Friendly
    • Your HELPING instead selling
    • Your the THE ONE

  • Understand who is the decision maker

  • Address your pitch the family not only to the caller

    • Understand what they like dislike
    • Ask what they have done in last holiday

  • Get to know your client requirement in more details, so only you will have this detail compared to other competitors

  • SELL A DREAM !


3. After the call - Do’s & Don'ts


Do’s Dont’s

  • After the call it's always recommended to send a thank you message to the customer via Twilio Frontline WhatsApp.

    • Please use the Twilio Frontline Template message “After Call Thank You”

  • If a customer don’t answer your call , DO NOT keeping trying calling. Instead send a WhatsApp message or email, asking for a good time to talk or missing information.

7. Customer Follow Up

  1. Follow up sequence

    1. After sending the tour packages with options, you next important stage is follow up. Most sales people fail in follow up and loose the sale. You have to do the right amount of follow up at the right time, using the right “follow up” approach. You need be patience and not push the client too much.

    2. Follow up with your customer daily, unless they specifically given you a date/time to follow up.

    3. After 30 days of follow up if there is no positive response, mark the status of the lead as “Not Live” unless you want to keep the lead in the live funnel with a reason.

    4. Follow up till the customer says don’t call me!

    5. Customer follow up can be done using whatsApp text messages or calls by using Twilio Frontline App

  2. Urgency

    1. A successful sale to happen you need to either create urgency or customer must have a natural urgency to book the trip. Example:

      1. Natural Urgency - customer got leave approved to a certain period and he must travel during that period and that period is not too far so must book fast. In this instant you don't need to create any urgency.

      2. Create Urgency - let's say if the customer has 12 more months for the travel date and they don't have urgency to make advance payment. In these cases you can create urgency by showing the customer that the rooms are getting filled and its good to book by paying 20%. Or you can use an air ticket price varying scenario to close the deal faster. If your creating urgency to the customer, make sure your client is accepting your urgency method. Else they might feel your trying to create unnecessary pressure.


You should know when to push the client



Do’s and Don'ts in Follow Up


Do’s Dont’s

  • If customers say negative feedback such as , I am busy right now, I have not gone through your proposal, I am waiting on my partners or my leave to be approved - all these answers are not helping you to move forward in the sales funnel. What you need to do, always ask the customer a date to follow up again. Eg. you can say “sir is it alright if I call you in 2 days to get an update”

  • Don’t always ask for payment updates. Eg. “Sir is there any update about your decision” instead you can send certain information to the client which will be beneficial. Eg. Vidoes, Testimonials, Pictures, Offers, Excursion Details.

  • Take notes and update the calling time in your CRM as a task under the customer or update in your personal calender.

  • Before calling the customer please check the timezone. Do NOT call the customer late night hours, in their time.

  • Always ask the customer what would be a good time period to catch up regarding the trip discussions. If they available after hours in their country, make sure you reach them around that time.

  • Follow up stage where customer is going to make the decision about the trip. So be nice, be professional and be friendly during your follow ups. Don't over do , also don't leave without proper follow ups.

DID YOU KNOW ?

Continues follow up is a good thing, it will help to plant idea to the customer that this business is important to you and you're paying your genuine attention.
